## What Is л°ӣм№Ё?

Every Hangul character is stacked inside a square block вҖ” and that block can have up to three layers.

```
  в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
  в”Ӯ   мҙҲм„ұ (initial)  в”Ӯ  вҶҗ consonant
  в”ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Ө
  в”Ӯ   мӨ‘м„ұ (middle)   в”Ӯ  вҶҗ vowel
  в”ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Ө
  в”Ӯ   мў…м„ұ (final)    в”Ӯ  вҶҗ consonant вҖ” this is the л°ӣм№Ё (batchim)
  в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
```

The consonant that sits in the final position is called **л°ӣм№Ё (batchim)**.

```
Without batchim (2 layers):   к°Җ [ka]  = г„ұ + г…Ҹ
With batchim (3 layers):      к°• [kaЕӢ] = г„ұ + г…Ҹ + г…Ү
```

Batchim is **optional** вҖ” it can be there or not. Most of the words from Weeks 1вҖ“4 had no batchim at all.

## There Are Only 7 Batchim Sounds

Here's the surprising part: all 19 Korean consonants can technically appear in the batchim position, but they only produce **exactly 7 distinct sounds**.

Multiple consonants "merge" into the **same sound** when they land in batchim position.

```
Sound      Consonants that produce it
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ     в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
[kМҡ]  вҶҗ   г„ұ  г…Ӣ  г„І
[n]   вҶҗ   г„ҙ
[tМҡ]  вҶҗ   г„·  г……  г…Ҷ  г…Ҳ  г…Ҡ  г…Ң  г…Һ
[l]   вҶҗ   г„№
[m]   вҶҗ   г…Ғ
[pМҡ]  вҶҗ   г…Ӯ  г…Қ
[ЕӢ]   вҶҗ   г…Ү
```

Those little Мҡ marks on [kМҡ] [tМҡ] [pМҡ] indicate **unreleased** stops. The batchim sound stops without a burst of air вҖ” think of holding the final consonant position without the small puff of air at the end вҖ” the sound stops dead.

```
English comparisons:
  [kМҡ]  вҶҗ  book, back, lock  (stop the k without releasing it)
  [tМҡ]  вҶҗ  boat, bit, heat   (stop the t without releasing it)
  [pМҡ]  вҶҗ  cup, stop, map    (stop the p without releasing it)
  [n]   вҶҗ  sun, man, on
  [l]   вҶҗ  ball, full, tell
  [m]   вҶҗ  room, dream, calm
  [ЕӢ]   вҶҗ  sing, ring, king
```

## г…Ү's Second Identity

What you learned in Week 2:

```
г…Ү as an initial consonant  вҶ’  silent [вҲ…]   e.g., м•„ = г…Ү + г…Ҹ вҶ’ [a]
```

What you're learning this week:

```
г…Ү as a final consonant  вҶ’  [ЕӢ] sound   e.g., к°• = г„ұ + г…Ҹ + г…Ү вҶ’ [kaЕӢ]
```

Same letter, completely different sound depending on position. Sneaky, right?

```
м•„мқҙ [a.i]       вҖ” two initial г…Ү's, both silent
к°•м•„м§Җ [kaЕӢ.a.dК‘i]  вҖ” the г…Ү in к°• is final вҶ’ [ЕӢ]; the г…Ү in м•„ is initial вҶ’ silent
```

For English speakers, [ЕӢ] is nothing new:
```
sing [sЙӘЕӢ]  /  ring [rЙӘЕӢ]  /  king [kЙӘЕӢ]
```
That sound at the end of those words? That's exactly the Korean final consonant г…Ү.

## Linking (м—°мқҢ): When Batchim Slides to the Next Syllable

When a syllable with batchim is followed by a syllable that **starts with a vowel**, the batchim moves over and becomes the initial consonant of that next syllable.

A "syllable starting with a vowel" is really just a syllable with **г…Ү (silent) as its initial consonant**. Since that г…Ү makes no sound, the preceding batchim steps in to fill the slot.

```
Written form    вҶ’    Pronunciation
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ          в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ
лӢ«м•„       вҶ’    [ta.da]       вҶҗ г„· batchim slides into the initial position of 'м•„'
л°Ҙмқҙ       вҶ’    [pa.bi]       вҶҗ г…Ӯ batchim slides into the initial position of 'мқҙ'
мӮ°мқҙ       вҶ’    [sa.ni]       вҶҗ г„ҙ batchim slides into the initial position of 'мқҙ'
к°•м•„м§Җ     вҶ’    [kaЕӢ.a.dК‘i]  вҶҗ г…Ү batchim also slides вҶ’ pronounced [ЕӢ] as the initial of 'м•„'
```

Note: the spelling doesn't change вҖ” only the pronunciation connects.

```
Common dictation traps:
  You hear [pa.bi] вҶ’ tempted to write л°”л№„ (X)   but the actual word is вҶ’ л°Ҙмқҙ (O)
  You hear [лӢӨ.лӢӨ] вҶ’ tempted to write лӢӨлӢӨ (X)   but the actual word is вҶ’ лӢ«м•„ (O)
```

## кІ№л°ӣм№Ё: Two Consonants in the Batchim Slot

Until now, every batchim has been a single consonant. But sometimes **two consonants** can sit together in the batchim position. This is called **кІ№л°ӣм№Ё** (double batchim).

Two consonants at once sounds alarming вҖ” but if you look closely, they're all made up of letters you already know.

```
лӢӯ  [takМҡ]    =  г„· + г…Ҹ + г„ә   (г„ә = г„№ + г„ұ)   chicken
мӮ¶  [sam]     =  г…… + г…Ҹ + г„»   (г„» = г„№ + г…Ғ)   life
м—ҶлӢӨ [КҢpМҡ.tНҲa] =  г…Ү + г…“ + г…„   (г…„ = г…Ӯ + г……)   to not exist
```

There are 11 double batchim combinations in Korean, but only a handful show up regularly in everyday use.

Here we'll focus on the **5 most common double batchim: г„ә, г„», г„ј, г„ҫ, г…„**.

### The Rule: Only One Gets Pronounced

So how do you actually say a double batchim? Do you race through both consonants at lightning speed? Nope. Only **one** of the two consonants is pronounced.

```
At the end of a word or before another consonant вҖ” only one sounds:
  лӢӯ [takМҡ]     вҖ” of г„ә, only г„ұ sounds (г„№ drops)
  мӮ¶ [sam]      вҖ” of г„», only г…Ғ sounds (г„№ drops)
  м—¬лҚҹ [jКҢ.dКҢl] вҖ” of г„ј, only г„№ sounds (г…Ӯ drops)
  м—ҶлӢӨ [КҢpМҡ.tНҲa]  вҖ” of г…„, only г…Ӯ sounds (г…… drops)
```

When a vowel follows (a syllable starting with silent г…Ү), linking kicks in and both consonants get to make a sound:

```
  лӢӯмқҙ [tal.gi]   вҖ” г„№ moves to the initial of 'мқҙ', г„ұ stays as the batchim of 'лӢӯ'
  мӮ¶мқҙ [sal.mi]   вҖ” г„№ moves to the initial of 'мқҙ', г…Ғ stays as the batchim of 'мӮ¶'
  м—Ҷм–ҙ [КҢpМҡ.sНҲКҢ]  вҖ” г…Ӯ stays as the batchim sound, г…… moves to the initial of 'м–ҙ'
```

*(Why does the г…… in м—Ҷм–ҙ come out as [sНҲ] (tense)? That's due to Korean's tensification rule вҖ” we'll cover that in a later week. For now, just remember that м—Ҷм–ҙ sounds like [м—…мҚЁ].)*

### The 5 Most Common Double Batchim

| Double Batchim | Components | Sound at Word End | Example |
|----------------|------------|-------------------|---------|
| г„ә | г„№+г„ұ | [kМҡ] | лӢӯ [takМҡ] (chicken) |
| г„» | г„№+г…Ғ | [m] | мӮ¶ [sam] (life) |
| г„ј | г„№+г…Ӯ | [l] | м—¬лҚҹ [jКҢ.dКҢl] (eight) |
| г„ҫ | г„№+г…Ң | [l] | н•ҘлӢӨ [hal.tНҲa] (to lick) |
| г…„ | г…Ӯ+г…… | [pМҡ] | м—ҶлӢӨ [КҢpМҡ.tНҲa] (to not exist) |

## Today's Writing Practice: Characters With Batchim

When batchim is present, the vowel in the middle layer gets compressed upward, making room for the batchim below.

### к°• [kaЕӢ] вҖ” batchim г…Ү [ЕӢ]

```
  в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”¬в”Җв”Җв”җ
  в”Ӯ г„ұ  в”Ӯг…Ҹв”Ӯ   вҶҗ initial + vowel (vertical vowel layout)
  в”ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҙв”Җв”Җв”Ө
  в”Ӯ   г…Ү   в”Ӯ   вҶҗ final consonant (batchim) вҖ” [ЕӢ] sound
  в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ

Stroke order: г„ұ(в‘ вҶ’ в‘ЎвҶ“) + г…Ҹ(в‘ўвҶ“ в‘ЈвҶ’) + г…Ү(в‘Ө circle)
```

### лӢ¬ [tal] вҖ” batchim г„№ [l]

```
  в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”¬в”Җв”Җв”җ
  в”Ӯ г„·  в”Ӯг…Ҹв”Ӯ   вҶҗ initial + vowel
  в”ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҙв”Җв”Җв”Ө
  в”Ӯ   г„№   в”Ӯ   вҶҗ final consonant (batchim) вҖ” [l] sound
  в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
```

### лҙ„ [pom] вҖ” batchim г…Ғ [m]

```
  в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
  в”Ӯ   г…Ӯ      в”Ӯ   вҶҗ initial consonant
  в”ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Ө
  в”Ӯ   г…—      в”Ӯ   вҶҗ vowel (horizontal vowel вҖ” sits below the consonant)
  в”ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Ө
  в”Ӯ   г…Ғ      в”Ӯ   вҶҗ final consonant (batchim) вҖ” [m] sound
  в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ

Stroke order: г…Ӯ(в‘ вҶ“ в‘ЎвҶ“ в‘ўвҶ’ в‘ЈвҶ’) + г…—(в‘ӨвҶ’ в‘ҘвҶ‘) + г…Ғ(в‘Ұ rectangle)
```

*(Horizontal vowels like г…—В·г…ңВ·г…Ў sit **below** the consonant rather than to the right вҖ” different from the layout in characters like к°• or лӢ¬, which use vertical vowels.)*

### л°Ҙ [papМҡ] вҖ” batchim г…Ӯ [pМҡ]

## What You Learned This Week

- [x] л°ӣм№Ё (batchim / final consonant) is the consonant that sits in the bottom layer of a Hangul block
- [x] Regardless of which consonant is written, batchim always produces one of **7 sounds**
- [x] Initial г…Ү is silent [вҲ…]; final г…Ү is [ЕӢ] вҖ” same letter, two totally different sounds
- [x] Linking (м—°мқҢ): when batchim is followed by a vowel, it slides over to become the initial consonant of the next syllable
- [x] Double batchim (кІ№л°ӣм№Ё): two consonants in the batchim slot вҖ” only one gets pronounced
- [x] The 5 most common double batchim: г„ә[kМҡ] г„»[m] г„ј[l] г„ҫ[l] г…„[pМҡ]
